## New dashboard look

The dashboard has been redesigned to make better use of screen space and to be easier to navigate. It's now wider and takes advantage of more of your monitor, while servers have moved to the sidebar on the left. The name of the selected module has been moved into the navigation bar, so you can always see where you are.

The sidebar itself has become narrower, cleaner, and more readable. We've added a search box that lets you quickly find the module you need, which is especially useful with longer lists. Prime modules now have a special purple accent, so they stand out at a glance.

The way modules are toggled has also changed. The previous switches have been replaced with dots on the left side: green means the module is enabled, gray means it's disabled. In addition, the name of a disabled module is dimmer, making it easy to tell at a glance what's active and what's not.

## Settings: New permissions

The permissions section has been completely moved into settings and rewritten from scratch. You can now grant access to specific users as well as entire roles, and the built-in search lets you quickly find the right person or role even on a large server. This makes managing who has access to the dashboard simpler and more flexible than before.

We've also refreshed the buttons in the danger zone. The "Reset levels" and "Reset economy" actions have a new style that more clearly signals these are irreversible operations.

## Commands: Multiple prefixes

The bot recognizes commands by the configured prefix, that is, the character that starts the message. By default you can set one such prefix, and servers with a Prime membership can define up to three. The bot will respond to each of them, so for example both `-help` and `.help` will trigger the same command.

Independently of that, we've added an option to use a mention as a prefix. Once enabled, you can invoke the bot by mentioning it at the start of a message, for example `@Bot work`. This is useful when someone doesn't remember the configured symbols but knows the bot can be summoned with a mention.
